Super Mario 64 Blog

History

Super Mario 64 (or SM64, for short) is a platform game developed and published by Nintendo for the Nintendo 64 console. It was released in both North America and Japan in 1996 and for the PAL regions in 1997. It's the first Super Mario game to feature 3D gameplay that combines traditional Super Mario gameplay, visual style, and characters in a large open world.


Gameplay

Super Mario 64 is a 3D platformer in which the player controls Mario through various courses. Mario's abilities are far more diverse than in previous games. He can walk, jump, crouch, crawl, climb, swim, kick, grab objects, and punch using the game controller's analog stick and buttons. He can also execute special jumps by combining a regular jump with other actions, including the double jump and triple jumps, long jump, backflip, and wall jump. The player can adjust the camera—operated by Lakitu—and toggle between first-person and third-person view.


Health, lives, and power-ups

Unlike many of its predecessors, Super Mario 64 uses a health point system, represented by a pie shape consisting of 8 segments. If Mario has taken damage, he can replenish his health by either collecting three types of coins—yellow, which replenishes one segment; red, equal to two yellow coins; and blue, which are equal to 5 yellow coins—or by walking through a spinning heart. However, if he is underwater, then his health is represented by how long he can hold his breath, which slowly diminishes while underwater and replenishes while above the surface. Instead of the typical power-ups found in other games, such as fire flowers and Super Mushroom, there are three colored blocks. Those being red, green, and blue; all of which have three switches of the same colors. The red block contains the wing cap which allows Mario to fly, the green block that contains the metal cap, which makes him immune to enemies, fire, noxious gases, etc. and the blue block contains the vanish cap, which makes Mario invisible.
